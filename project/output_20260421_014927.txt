### Significant Nuclear Accidents

1. **Chernobyl (1986)**

Chernobyl was a catastrophic nuclear disaster that occurred on April 26, 1986, at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, located in Ukraine. The accident was caused by a combination of human error, design flaws, and a lack of safety procedures.

* Causes:
	+ Human error: Operators made a series of critical mistakes during a safety test, which led to a power surge and a station blackout.
	+ Design flaws: The RBMK reactor design made it prone to power surges and lack of containment structures.
	+ Lack of safety procedures: Inadequate training, inadequate safety procedures, and a culture of secrecy contributed to the accident.
* Consequences:
	+ Release of radioactive materials into the environment, contaminating a large area around the plant.
	+ Evacuation of over 100,000 people from the surrounding area.
	+ High radiation exposure for nearby residents and emergency responders.
	+ Estimated 28 immediate deaths, with estimates of up to 93,000 long-term cancer deaths.
* Lessons learned:
	+ Importance of operator training and proper safety procedures.
	+ Need for robust containment structures and emergency core cooling systems.
	+ Importance of transparency and public communication in nuclear safety.
